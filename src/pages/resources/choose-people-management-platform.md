# Lattice vs 15Five vs Culture Amp vs Leapsome: How to Choose a People-Management Platform

If you're comparing Lattice, 15Five, Culture Amp, and Leapsome, you're already past the "do we need software for this" stage. You have managers running 1:1s inconsistently, goals living in slide decks, engagement data nobody acts on, or a performance process held together with spreadsheets — and you want one system to carry more of the load.

Here's the uncomfortable truth most comparison pages won't tell you: **all four of these platforms are credible, and each one is the genuinely better choice for a specific kind of buyer.** So is Cadence, which we make — and so we'll tell you exactly where we fit and where we don't, including the cases where you should buy a competitor.

This guide gives you a criteria-driven way to decide, not a scoreboard rigged for the vendor writing it.

## Start with your problem, not the feature list

Every platform in this category will demo well. The feature lists overlap heavily: all of them now claim some combination of 1:1s, goals, surveys, feedback, analytics, and AI assistance. Choosing on feature-count is how teams end up with expensive shelfware.

Instead, name the primary problem you're solving. It's usually one of these:

| Your primary problem | What you actually need | Start your shortlist with |
|---|---|---|
| Performance reviews and compensation cycles are chaotic | A mature performance and talent suite with comp workflows | Lattice, Leapsome |
| Managers are inconsistent and undersupported | Manager enablement, check-in rhythm, coaching support | 15Five, Cadence |
| You can't see or measure engagement and culture | Survey depth and people-science rigor | Culture Amp |
| You want one broad HR suite, including learning | A modular all-in-one people platform | Leapsome |
| Management work is scattered across disconnected tools | A connected management record across 1:1s, goals, recognition, ER, and surveys | Cadence |

That table is the honest version of this entire article. The rest is the detail behind it.

## What each platform is genuinely good at

These descriptions are based on each vendor's own public positioning. Verify current specifics on their sites — this category moves fast.

### Lattice: performance and talent-suite breadth

Lattice publicly offers a broad performance and talent suite: 1:1s, goals and OKRs, engagement surveys, talent reviews, career growth tracks, compensation management, analytics, and an AI agent for summarization, coaching, analysis, and note-taking. If your buying trigger is "our review cycle and comp process need a real system," Lattice's breadth in performance and talent workflows is a legitimate reason it shows up on most shortlists.

**Lattice is likely your best fit if:** formal performance reviews, talent reviews, and compensation cycles are the center of your requirements, and you want them from one established suite.

### 15Five: manager effectiveness and engagement rhythm

15Five's public positioning centers on making managers better. Its product families cover performance (reviews, 1:1s, OKRs, a talent matrix, recognition via High Fives) and engagement (surveys, heat maps, action plans), plus Kona, its AI coach and meeting assistant that works alongside check-ins, reviews, and 1:1s. The through-line is manager enablement: a regular check-in cadence with coaching support wrapped around it.

**15Five is likely your best fit if:** your core thesis is "improve the managers and the rest follows," and you want an established check-in-driven system with manager coaching built around it.

### Culture Amp: engagement and people-science depth

Culture Amp positions itself as an employee-experience platform grounded in people science — engagement surveys with an extensive template library, AI-assisted comment analysis, analytics, and performance and development modules, all framed as an intelligence layer connecting culture and performance. If measurement rigor matters — benchmarking, survey design quality, defensible methodology — Culture Amp is the reference point the rest of the category gets compared to.

**Culture Amp is likely your best fit if:** understanding engagement and culture with research-grade rigor is your primary need, and survey depth outweighs workflow tooling in your evaluation.

### Leapsome: broad, modular all-in-one suite

Leapsome publicly offers a modular people platform spanning performance reviews, engagement surveys, goals and OKRs, learning, 1:1s and team meetings, instant feedback and praise, compensation, calibration tooling, AI capabilities, and workflow automation. It has strong roots in the European market. The pitch is configurability: pick the modules you need, run a lot of your people processes in one place.

**Leapsome is likely your best fit if:** you want the widest module coverage from a single vendor — including learning and compensation — and you value configuring a suite to your existing processes.

### Cadence: the connected management record

Cadence takes a different cut at the problem. Instead of leading with review cycles or survey science, Cadence is a management operating plane: the recurring manager–employee 1:1 is the operating record, and it's connected to goals and OKR cascading, recognition, employee-relations case context, pulse surveys, job architecture, and a Culture Scorecard with manager and org-health views. AI generates meeting summaries and private coaching for managers and, on Professional plans, for employees too (where enabled) — but AI never makes people decisions. Humans own promotions, ratings, discipline, and compensation decisions, full stop.

Two things we do that are worth copying into your evaluation of every vendor, including us: we label everything on our site as live, Preview, or Coming with a date — and we don't count roadmap items as reasons to buy today.

**Cadence is likely your best fit if:** your problem is fragmentation — management work scattered across tools that share no data — and you want the day-to-day management rhythm (not the annual review cycle) to be the system of record.

## Where Cadence is honestly not the right choice

We'd rather tell you now than lose you at renewal:

- **You need compensation management today.** Cadence doesn't do comp. Lattice and Leapsome both publicly offer compensation modules. If comp cycles are in scope, they belong on your shortlist and we don't.
- **Survey science is your primary requirement.** Cadence's pulse surveys capture responses and aggregate analytics, and they feed the management record — but Culture Amp is the survey and people-science leader. We won't pretend otherwise.
- **You need a learning management system.** Leapsome includes learning modules. Cadence doesn't.
- **You need an HRIS.** None of these five platforms is one, but to be explicit: Cadence is not an HRIS and doesn't try to be.
- **You need SCIM provisioning right now.** Cadence Enterprise ships SSO via OIDC today; SAML and SCIM are on our Q4 roadmap. If SCIM is a hard requirement this quarter, that matters.

## The criteria matrix: what to actually evaluate

Score every vendor — including us — on these seven criteria. Weight them by your problem, not the vendor's demo script.

| Criterion | The question to ask | What good looks like |
|---|---|---|
| 1. System of record | Where does the management work actually live — in this tool, or in docs and DMs around it? | One connected record across 1:1s, goals, recognition, and people signals, not modules that share a login but not context |
| 2. Manager adoption | Will an average manager use this weekly without being chased? | The tool fits the existing management rhythm (1:1s, check-ins) instead of adding a parallel process |
| 3. AI governance | Does AI support decisions or make them? What data trains the models? | AI drafts, summarizes, and coaches; humans decide. Clear written answer on training data. |
| 4. Live vs. roadmap | Which demoed capabilities are in production today for customers like you? | The vendor labels live, preview, and roadmap explicitly — and will show any capability in their production product |
| 5. Coverage vs. depth | Does it cover your top three workflows deeply, or ten workflows thinly? | Depth on your primary problem; honesty about what it doesn't do |
| 6. Sensitive-workflow handling | Where do ER cases, PIPs, and difficult conversations live? | Documented, access-controlled context connected to the management record — or a clear answer that it's out of scope |
| 7. Total cost across the stack | What does the whole stack cost once you add the tools this doesn't replace? | Per-seat price plus the point tools you'll still be paying for |

## Questions to ask every vendor (including Cadence)

Take these into every sales call. The answers separate the category leaders from the demo-ware.

1. **"Show me this in your production product, not a demo environment."** Any capability that materially drives your decision should be demonstrable in prod. If a vendor hesitates, treat that capability as roadmap.
2. **"Which of the features you just showed me are generally available, in preview, or on the roadmap — and can I get that in writing?"** Cadence's answer: every capability on our site carries a live, Preview, or Coming label. As of this writing, for example, our consented working-style profiles (Personality thin v1) are live on Professional, while the deeper Personality coaching surface is Coming Q3 (August 15) and is not included in current purchase access. Ask everyone for that same level of specificity.
3. **"Is my company's data used to train your AI models?"** Cadence's answer: no — customer data is not used to train Cadence AI models by default, and only would be if your company explicitly agreed in writing. Get every vendor's answer in the contract, not the sales deck.
4. **"Can your AI ever produce a rating, ranking, or decision about a person on its own?"** Cadence's answer: no. AI outputs are decision support; discipline, promotion, termination, compensation, and performance ratings stay human. Push every vendor to be equally specific.
5. **"What happens to manager adoption after month three?"** Ask for their honest view of what drives weekly usage versus cycle-driven usage (tools used twice a year at review time).
6. **"What's your security and compliance posture, exactly?"** Ask for the DPA and current certification status. Cadence's answer: DPA available for review; SOC 2 readiness is in progress — we won't claim a certification we don't hold yet, and you should be suspicious of vague answers here from anyone.

## A decision path that holds up

1. **Write down your top three problems** in plain language before any demo.
2. **Shortlist two or three vendors** using the problem table above — not all five.
3. **Run the six vendor questions** and keep written answers.
4. **Pilot with real managers,** not just HR. Weekly-use tools live or die on manager adoption.
5. **Price the full stack,** including the tools each option doesn't replace.
6. **Re-check roadmap claims** before signing. Anything not live in production is a bet, whoever the vendor is.

## FAQ

**Do all of these platforms have AI features now?**
Yes. Lattice, 15Five, Culture Amp, and Leapsome all publicly market AI capabilities — assistants, coaching, comment analysis, or agents — and so does Cadence. The differentiator isn't "has AI"; it's what the AI is connected to, what data trains it, and whether it supports or replaces human decisions. Evaluate on those three, not the AI label.

**Can Cadence replace Lattice for performance reviews and compensation?**
Not if compensation is in scope — Cadence doesn't do comp management, and Lattice does. Cadence's center of gravity is the connected day-to-day management record (1:1s, goals, recognition, ER context, surveys, culture scorecard) rather than a full comp-and-review suite. Some teams run Cadence alongside dedicated comp tooling.

**Which platform is best for a 100–300 person company?**
The one that matches your primary problem — size alone doesn't decide it. At that range, weight manager adoption heavily: whichever tool your managers actually use weekly will beat a broader suite they ignore. Cadence's published fit at that size is Essentials ($12/seat/month, billed annually) or Professional ($24/seat/month, billed annually), self-serve.

**How should we handle roadmap promises during evaluation?**
Price and decide on what's live in production today, and ask every vendor to label the rest with dates in writing. Cadence publishes live/Preview/Coming labels for exactly this reason — hold us and everyone else to the same test.
